Our Three Main Breeds of Dragons

Red Dragons

Red dragons are hot tempered and proud. They are not recommended for beginners. A red dragon is a lifelong commitment, as they will live a healthy lifespan of 40 years if properly cared for. Red dragons are exclusively carnivorous.

Black Dragons

Black dragons are on the smaller side, but highly intelligent. Some breeders have described them as having a "cat like" temperament. Black dragons are one of the easier breeds to care for, but have expensive dietary needs. They should mostly be fed fresh fish.

Gold Dragons

Gold dragons are highly affectionate, but often overly protective. Owners should take care to acclimate them to strangers slowly. Gold dragons make excellent guard-dragons as long as they are properly cared for and trained.
